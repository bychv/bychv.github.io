Gauge(Transform) Invariance

$$\left\{ \begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{A}'&=\boldsymbol{A}+\nabla\chi\\ \phi'&=\phi-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\chi}{\partial t} \end{aligned} \right. $$

$$ \begin{aligned} \boldsymbol{E}'&=-\nabla\phi'-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{A}'}{\partial t} \\ &=-\nabla\bigg(\phi-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\chi}{\partial t}\bigg)-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial}{\partial t}(\boldsymbol{A}+\nabla\chi)\\ &=-\nabla\phi+\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\chi}{\partial t}-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{A}}{\partial t}-\nabla\bigg(\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\chi}{\partial t}\bigg)\\ &=-\nabla\phi-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{A}}{\partial t}\\ &=\boldsymbol{E} \end{aligned} $$

After the gauge transformation, the electric field remains the same.

$$\boldsymbol{B}'=\nabla\times(\boldsymbol{A}+\nabla\chi)=\nabla\times\boldsymbol{A}=\boldsymbol{B} $$

The magnetic field remains the same after the gauge transformation.

Gauge: Coloumb Gauge

$$\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{A}=0 $$

and $ \phi=0 $,call radiation gauge.

Gauge: Lorenz Gauge

$$\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{A}+\frac{1}{c^2}\frac{\partial\phi}{\partial t}=0 $$

Multipole Expansion

$$W_e=-\boldsymbol{d}\cdot\boldsymbol{E} $$

d is the eletric-dipole moment.

$$\boldsymbol{d}=q\boldsymbol{x} $$

$$W_m=-\boldsymbol{\mu}\cdot\boldsymbol{B} $$

$$\boldsymbol{\mu}=\frac{q\boldsymbol{L}}{2mc} $$

Canonical Quantization

a) Viraable: cannonical
position $\boldsymbol{x}$, momentum $\boldsymbol{p}$

$$A(\boldsymbol{x},\boldsymbol{p})\to\hat{A}(\hat{x},\hat{q}) $$

b) Commutation relation
CM:

$$\begin{align} [x_i,p_j]_{PB}&=\delta_{ij} \\ [x_i,x_j]_{PB}&=[p_i,p_j]_{PB}=0 \end{align} $$

QM:

$$\begin{align} [\hat{x}_i,\hat{p}_j]&=\i\hbar\delta_{ij} \\ [\hat{x}_i,\hat{x}_j]&=[\hat{p}_i,\hat{p}_j]=0 \end{align} $$

Properties of $\boldsymbol{e}_{i,j,k}$

  1. Othonomality

$$\boldsymbol{e}_i\cdot\boldsymbol{e}_j=\delta_{ij} $$

  1. Completeness

$$\sum_{i=1}^{3}\boldsymbol{e}_i\boldsymbol{e}_i=\mathbb{1}\quad(\text{并矢}) $$
