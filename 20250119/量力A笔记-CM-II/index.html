量子力学

$$\require{amssymb} \newcommand{\d}{\mathrm{d}} \newcommand{\i}{\mathrm{i}} \newcommand{\oiint}{\subset\kern{-3pt}\supset\kern{-16.5pt}\iint} $$

Vector Analysis

Use Enstein Convention.
$\vec{x}= x_i\vec{e}_i$
inner product

$$\vec{e}_i\cdot\vec{e}_j=\delta_{ij}\quad (i,j=1,2,3) $$

$\delta_{ij}$ is Kronecker Delta.
cross product

$$\vec{e}_i\times\vec{e}_j=\epsilon_{ijk}\vec{e}_k \quad (i,j,k=1,2,3) $$

$\epsilon_{ijk}$ is Levi-Civita Symbol.

Properties of $\delta_{ij}$ & $\epsilon_{ijk}$

Symmetry and Anti-symmetry.

$$\delta_{ij}=\delta_{ji} $$

$$\epsilon_{ijk}=-\epsilon_{jik} $$

Defination of $\delta_{ij}$ & $\epsilon_{ijk}$

$$\delta_{ij}= \begin{cases} 1 & i=j \\ 0 & i\neq j \end{cases} $$

Orhtogonormality.

$$\epsilon_{ijk}= \begin{cases} 1 & \text{even permutation} \\ -1 & \text{odd permutation} \\ 0 & \text{no permutation} \end{cases} $$

if $F_{ij}=F{ji}$, then $\epsilon_{ijk}F_{ij}\equiv 0$

$$F_i\delta_{ij}=F_j $$

Vector Algebra

$\forall \vec{A},\vec{B}$, $\vec{A}=A_i\vec{e}_i$, $\vec{B}=B_i\vec{e}_i$

$$\begin{align} \vec{A}\cdot\vec{B} &= (A_i\vec{e}_i)\cdot(B_j\vec{e}_j) \\ &= (A_iB_j)(\vec{e}_i\cdot\vec{e}_j) \\ &= A_iB_i\delta_{ij} \\ &= A_iB_i \end{align} $$

$$\begin{align} \vec{A}\times\vec{B} &= (A_i\vec{e}_i)\times(B_j\vec{e}_j) \\ &= (A_iB_j)(\vec{e}_i\times\vec{e}_j) \\ &= A_iB_i\epsilon_{ijk}\vec{e}_k \\ &= \vec{e}_k(A_iB_i\epsilon_{kij}) \\ &= \vec{e}_k(\vec{A}\times\vec{B})_k \end{align} $$

$$\boxed{ (\vec{A}\times\vec{B})_i = \epsilon_{ijk}A_jB_k } $$

$$\epsilon_{ijk}= \vec{e}_i\cdot(\vec{e}_j\times\vec{e}_k) $$

$$\epsilon_{ijk}\epsilon_{lmn}= \begin{vmatrix} \delta_{il} & \delta_{im} & \delta_{in} \\ \delta_{jl} & \delta_{jm} & \delta_{jn} \\ \delta_{kl} & \delta_{km} & \delta_{kn} \\ \end{vmatrix} $$

$$\boxed{\epsilon_{ijk}\epsilon_{klm}= \delta_{il}\delta_{jm}-\delta_{im}\delta_{jl}} $$

$$\vec{a}\cdot(\vec{b}\times\vec{c})=\vec{b}\cdot(\vec{c}\times\vec{a})=\vec{c}\cdot(\vec{a}\times\vec{b}) $$

important formula

$$\nabla(uv)=u\nabla v+v\nabla u $$

$$\nabla\times(\nabla\times\vec{A)})=\nabla(\nabla\cdot\vec{A})-\nabla^2\vec{A} $$

$$\nabla\cdot(\nabla\times\phi)\equiv0 $$

$$\nabla\times(\nabla\vec{A})\equiv0 $$

$$\nabla^2\bigg(\frac{1}{r}\bigg)=4\pi\delta(\vec{r}) $$

$$\nabla\bigg(\frac{1}{r}\bigg)=-\frac{\vec{r}}{r^3} $$

Generalized Stokes Theorem

$$\int_{\partial\Omega} \omega = \int_{\Omega} \d\omega $$

  1. Newton-Leibniz Formula

$$\int_{a}^{b} f'(x)\d x = \int_{a}^{b} \d f(x) = f(b)-f(a) $$

  1. Green’s Theorem

$$\oint f\d x+g\d y = \iint\limits_{D} \bigg(\frac{\partial g}{\partial x}-\frac{\partial f}{\partial y}\bigg)\d x\d y $$

  1. Gauss-Ostrogradsky Theorem

$$\oiint_\Sigma \vec{F}\cdot\d\vec{\Sigma} = \int_V \nabla\cdot\vec{F}\d V $$

$$\oiint_\Sigma \psi\d\vec{\Sigma} = \int_V \nabla\psi\d V $$

$$\oiint_\Sigma \d\vec{\Sigma}\times\vec{A} = \int_V \Big(\nabla\times\vec{A}\Big)\d V $$

  1. Kelvin-Stokes Theorem

$$\oint_L \vec{F}\cdot\d\vec{l} = \iint\limits_{\Sigma} \Big(\nabla\times\vec{F}\Big)\cdot\d\vec{\Sigma} $$

Helmholtz Theorem

$\forall \vec{F}$,contitinuous and differentiable,

$$\vec{F}=\vec{F}_\parallel+\vec{F}_\perp $$

$$\left\{\begin{align} \nabla\times\vec{F}_\parallel &\equiv 0 \\ \nabla\cdot\vec{F}_\perp &\equiv 0 \end{align}\right. $$

$$\cases{ \vec{F}_\parallel = -\nabla\phi \\ \vec{F}_\perp = \nabla\times\vec{A} } $$

Experimental laws

Coulomb’s Law

$$\begin{align} \boldsymbol{E}(\boldsymbol{r})&=\int\frac{\rho(\boldsymbol{r'})}{|\boldsymbol{r}-\boldsymbol{r'}|^3}(\boldsymbol{r}-\boldsymbol{r'})\d^3 \boldsymbol{r'}\\ &=-\nabla\phi \end{align} $$

$$\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{E}=4\pi\rho(\boldsymbol{r}) $$

Biot-Savart’s Law

$$\begin{align} \boldsymbol{B}(\boldsymbol{r})&=\frac{1}{c}\int\frac{\boldsymbol{J}(\boldsymbol{r'})\times(\boldsymbol{r}-\boldsymbol{r'})}{|\boldsymbol{r}-\boldsymbol{r'}|^3}\d^3 \boldsymbol{r'}\\ &=\nabla\times\boldsymbol{A} \end{align} $$

$$\nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{B}=0 $$

Ampere’s Law (Circuit)

$$\oint\boldsymbol{B}\cdot\d\boldsymbol{l}=\frac{4\pi}{c}\iint_{\Sigma}\boldsymbol{J}\cdot\d\boldsymbol{\Sigma} $$

$$\nabla\times\boldsymbol{B}=\frac{4\pi}{c}\boldsymbol{J}+\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{E}}{\partial t} $$

Faraday’s Law (Induction)

$$\oint\boldsymbol{E}\cdot\d\boldsymbol{l}=-\frac{1}{c}\iint_{\Sigma}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{B}}{\partial t}\cdot\d\boldsymbol{\Sigma} $$

$$\nabla\times\boldsymbol{E}=-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{B}}{\partial t} $$

Continuity law of eletric charge

$ \frac{\d Q}{\d t}=0 $ , $ Q=\int_{V}\rho(\boldsymbol{r},t)\d^3\boldsymbol{r}$

$$\Rightarrow \nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{J}+\frac{\partial\rho}{\partial t}=0 $$

Maxwell’s Equation

$$\left\{\begin{align} \nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{E}&=4\pi\rho \\ \nabla\cdot\boldsymbol{B}&=0 \\ \nabla\times\boldsymbol{E}&=-\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{B}}{\partial t} \\ \nabla\times\boldsymbol{B}&=\frac{4\pi}{c}\boldsymbol{J}+\frac{1}{c}\frac{\partial\boldsymbol{E}}{\partial t} \end{align}\right. $$

Lorentz Force

$$\boldsymbol{F}=q(\boldsymbol{E}+\boldsymbol{v}\times\boldsymbol{B}) $$
